Mixing

Any time that whisky has achieved popularity has been when it’s been diluted. Toddies, Slings, grog, Juleps, the Whisky Punch and the Highball are the drinks that elevated whisky from being a burning, neat spirit drunk for effect into something that people enjoyed consuming. The same applies today. The idea that it should only ever be consumed neat is a new phenomenon. So, if anyone - friend, bartender, or writer - orders you to drink whisky neat, reach for that bottle of water, my friend, and pour it over their hand - saving some for your dram.

WATER

It not only helps you to (re)hydrate, but is also an essential companion when enjoying whisky. Not only does adding a drop of water when nosing and tasting allow you to appreciate fully whisky’s aromas, but dilution is good when drinking

GINGER ALE

The addition of bubbles to ginger-infused water produces whisky’s finest mixer, as the ginger is a natural partner with the sweet spices in many whiskies. Importantly, ginger also extend the finish of any whisky.

COKE

Coke’s ubiquity as a soft drink has helped it become the go-to mixer for many spirits, and whisky is no exception. It was coke that elevated blended Scotch to mass-market appeal in Spain in the 1990s.

GREEN TEA

Green Tea - Green tea is the most common version of this mixer to be used, but the high sugar levels of some brand alter the balance of the drink, making it too saccharine. Sugar is important as green tea will have a dry grip that combines with the oak in the whisky
